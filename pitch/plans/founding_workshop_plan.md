# Founding Workshop Presentation Plan
## Executive Summary for Morten & Henrik

**Purpose**: 2-hour founding workshop playbook to align on business model, contributions, and ownership structure

**Audience**: Co-founders Morten (delivery/methodology) and Henrik (sales/strategy) — first time seeing these documents

**Tone**: Straight to the point, friendly, concise. Lead softly with context.

**Design Principles** (to establish upfront):
- Build a highly successful business that can grow beyond a few employees
- Sufficient senior sales and delivery capacity to grow fast
- Sustained and aligned growth — no burnout, no gaps

---

## Proposed 10-Slide Structure

### OPENING (2 slides) — Set the Frame

**Slide 1: Title**
- "THE AGENTIC AGENCY — FOUNDING ALIGNMENT"
- "A realistic starting point for our partnership conversation"
- March 2026

> **Visual Design**: Use `title` slide type. Full cement background (#E6E6E1), large uppercase Space Grotesk headline (72pt). Clean, authoritative opening.

> **Talking Points**:
> - "This is the first time we're all looking at the same numbers together"
> - "I've built these models over the past weeks — today I want to walk you through them and hear your reactions"
> - "Nothing here is final. This is a starting point for negotiation, not a verdict"

> **Facilitation Note**: Open with warmth. Acknowledge that seeing financial models for the first time can feel exposing. Set the tone that this is collaborative, not confrontational.

**Slide 2: Design Principles**
- Statement slide: "What we're optimizing for"
- 3-4 principles:
  1. A business that scales beyond the three of us
  2. Senior sales + delivery capacity from Month 7
  3. Fast growth without founder burnout
  4. Aligned contributions → aligned ownership

> **Visual Design**: Use `content` slide type with `layout: cards` (3-column). Each principle in a bordered white card with thick black border and large typographic numbers.

> **Talking Points**:
> - Principle 1: "We're not building a lifestyle business. We need to scale past our own capacity."
> - Principle 2: "Oct-26 is when we need to have senior help or we hit a wall. The budget shows why."
> - Principle 3: "I'm seeing 100%+ utilization for myself in 14 weeks. Morten is at 120% in WS weeks. This isn't sustainable."
> - Principle 4: "Ownership should reflect what each of us actually contributes — not just cash, but time, risk, and deliverables."

> **Facilitation Note**: Pause after presenting. Ask: "Do these feel like the right principles to optimize for? Is anything missing?" Get buy-in on the frame before diving into numbers.

> **Exact Data Points to Cite**:

> - Henrik: sole closer for 6 months (Apr-Sep), no written targets

> **Facilitation Note**: This is the first tension point. Lead with "These aren't criticisms — they're constraints we need to solve together." If Morten gets defensive about 120%, acknowledge it: "That's exactly why the playbook handoff is so critical."

> **Potential Tension**: Morten may push back on 120% — "I can handle it" or "Where does that number come from?" Be ready to show the workshop schedule and hours calculation.

> **Backup Content** (if founders want to dive deeper):

### CONTRIBUTIONS (3 slides) — What Each Founder Delivers

**Slide 6: Daniel — Delivery Engine**

- 65% equity — most defensible (sole cash + delivery risk)
- Key obligation: survive the Oct-26 step change without burnout
- Sweat equity question: 80K vs 180-200K market rate

> **Visual Design**: Use `content` slide type with `layout: numbered`. Four numbered blocks. Strong visual hierarchy with largest number (01) for headline commitment.

> **Talking Points**:
> - "I'm putting in 1.5 FTE — full-time plus. And 80K per month in cash. That's the seed capital."
> - "At 65% equity, I'm the largest shareholder. Why? Because I carry sole cash risk and I'm the delivery engine after Morten transitions out."
> - "The sweat equity calculation: 80K/month vs 180-200K market rate for a senior consultant. That's 100-120K/month in unpaid labor. Over 18 months, that's 1.8-2.1M DKK in sweat equity."
> - "My test: Can I survive the Oct-26 step change without burning out? That's when I'm managing a senior hire, an AM, and still delivering."

> **Facilitation Note**: Be vulnerable here. "I want to be transparent about what I'm asking for and what I'm giving up." Model the behavior you want from the others.

> **Backup Content** (if founders want to dive deeper):
> - Market rate research for senior consultants in Copenhagen
> - Detailed time commitment breakdown by week
> - Risk analysis: what Daniel loses if the business fails

## Facilitation Playbook: Handling Tension

### If Morten pushes back on 120% utilization:
- Acknowledge: "That number feels high, and you know your capacity better than I do."
- Probe: "Help me understand — how many workshop days per week feels sustainable?"
- Redirect: "Regardless of the exact number, the principle stands: we need the playbook handoff to work so you can step back."

### If Henrik resists specific sales targets:
- Acknowledge: "I hear that targets feel constraining. What would you propose instead?"
- Probe: "If we get to October and sales are below plan, how would we know? What would we measure?"
- Redirect: "I need some way to assess whether sales are on track. What feels fair to you?"

### If either founder questions their equity share:
- Acknowledge: "Equity is always emotional. Let's talk about it."
- Probe: "What do you think would be fair, and why?"
- Redirect: "Let's start with contributions. If we agree on what each person delivers, the equity conversation becomes easier."

### If the conversation gets heated:
- Pause: "Let's take a breath. This is hard stuff."
- Normalize: "Disagreement is healthy. It means we're being honest."
- Reframe: "We're all trying to build something together. Let's find the path forward."

### If someone shuts down:
- Notice: "I'm sensing you might have more to say. Am I reading that right?"
- Invite: "What's on your mind?"
- Protect: "There's no wrong answer here. I genuinely want to understand your perspective."
